Understanding and Implementing Principal Components Analysis (in R)

20 January 2017

Prompted by a recent discussion with a fellow-graduate student, I've decided I'd write a quick post about the principal components analysis (henceforth, PCA) and how to implement it in R through a working example.

Without going into much detail (I'm assuming that if you're reading this post, you've at least heard of PCA), PCA is a dimensionality reduction technique that, as its name implies, seeks simplify complicated linear models by identifying the k components k or predictors p, among the full set of predictors p, that explain 99% of the variance in y. If k < p (and explains 99% of the variance in y), then the simpler model should be prefered over the more complicated model.

In contrast, if all predictors p are required to explain 99% of the variance—and thus if a subset of the predictors p fail to explain 99% of the variance in y—then the full model should be preferred over a simple model. Note that more complicated models necesarily will explain more of the variance in y than less complicated models simply by virtue of having more predictors, a phenomenon that statisticians and machine-learning researchers call "overfitting".

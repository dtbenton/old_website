Quick Update

26 June 2014

The purpose of this blog post is to provide a quick update given that I haven't written a proper post in about one month. There is good reason for this.

The first is that I wanted to focus more on research and scheduling infants for my studies, what with the fact that there aren't very many RAs in my lab at the moment to do much of the grunt work for me.

The second reason for the lacuna is because most of my energy—when I'm not doing research—has been spent teaching myself both linear algebra and differential equations for computational-modeling purposes. In total, it's taken me about one and a half months to learn both subjects.

Note that you need not learn neither linear algebra nor differential equations to model effectively problems of your own choosing. This decision to delve more deeply into these subjects was primarily the consequence of my desire to develop a deeper, and hence less superficial, understanding of the models that are, at least in some sense, predicated on these mathematical principles.

With the newly acquired free time that I now have, my hope is to write a few more modelling posts and perhaps even start another series, in which I'll discuss the methods that developmental psychologists exploit to explore infant cognition, time permitting.

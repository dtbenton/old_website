2014 Site Redesign

14 Mar 2014

In what has become something of a tradition around here, I've once again decided to redesign this site, this time with one eye toward responsivity and one toward minimalism, the latter of which constituting the most-recent decision.

Some of the more substantial changes I've made include turning the site into more of a blog. Given that I've really only ever used this site to write posts and to upload videos, I think this was a good design decision. You can, of course, judge for yourself, but I think I've done quite a brilliant job, what with the fact that the entire redesign took just under one day.

In addition to turning the site into more of a blog, I've adopted a less-is-more mentality and have given the site a minimalist design. I've made this decision for many reasons, one important reason being that I want this new version of the site to espouse more of a professional, clean impression.

The third and final change I've made is that this site will now be exclusively a science blog, in which I'll write about all things infancy research, computational modelling, and statistics. I figure that because I'm in graduate school, my aim should be to write about the things I am learning and the things I've so far learned.

N.B. I've decided for the time being to keep the dummy posts (found on the home page) until there's enough written material to replace them. I promise that I'll endeavor to work as quickly as possible to replace the dummy posts with (ideally) more interesting material. Pray, be patient!

—DB
